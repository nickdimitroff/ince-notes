## Basics of sound

- A sound wave is a disturbance in a medium, e.g., vibration in a solid or air pressure changes in air.
- The disturbance travels with a speed based on the medium's properties and mass.
- The air itself does not ove much, it oscillates in its general viscinity.
- The wave/disturbance moves, causing compressions/rarefactions of the air molecules in their local regions.

## SPL

For very simple sound signals (e.g., sine waves), amplitude can be represented by its peak or peak-to-peak amplitude. For complex signals, use RMS (Root Mean Square) amplitude.

0. Take a set of pressure readings over time
1. Square them (each reading becomes positive)
2. Take mean of those values
3. Take square root of that mean

RMS -> root of the mean of the square

## Rules of thumb

+/- 3dB --> just noticable and sound pressure is doubled/halved
+/- 10dB --> loudness doubled/halved

## Broadband and narrowband sound

Broadband sound has noise across the full frequency range. Narrowband is restricted to a narrow range of frequencies.
